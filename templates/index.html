FAQ
Common questions
CrackScan works best on concrete walls, asphalt roads, masonry structures, and bridge surfaces. The model was trained on the DeepCrack dataset which contains diverse structural crack images from real inspection scenarios.
Supports JPG, JPEG, PNG, and BMP formats up to 16 MB. Images are automatically resized to 256×256 for inference. For best results, use high-contrast, well-lit photos taken perpendicular to the surface.
Severity is determined by the crack coverage percentage: Low risk is under 5% coverage, Medium risk is 5–15% coverage, and High risk is anything above 15%. These thresholds are derived from standard structural inspection guidelines.
The threshold controls the minimum probability a pixel needs to be classified as cracked. Lower values (0.2–0.4) will catch more cracks including faint ones but may include false positives. Higher values (0.6–0.8) are more conservative and precise.
Images are processed only for the duration of your session and are not stored on any server. History is maintained only in your browser's current session and is cleared when you close the tab.
